Media Manager Help

Media Manager is your household's streaming hub for movies, TV shows, personal videos, live TV, and security cameras. This guide covers everything you can do as a viewer.

Home

The home page shows what's relevant to you right now.

Continue Watching

Titles you've started but haven't finished appear here with a progress bar and time remaining. Click to resume where you left off. Use the X button to dismiss a title you don't plan to finish.

Recently Added

The latest titles added to the library. Click any poster to see details or start watching.

New Seasons Available

TV shows in the library that have new seasons you don't own yet. This helps you know when it's time to pick up the next season of a show you follow. Dismiss individual shows if you're not interested.

Browsing Your Library

Movies & TV Shows

Browse the full catalog from the Movies or TV Shows tabs. Each page offers:

  • Playable filter — show only titles ready for streaming (transcoded for your browser).
  • Rating filter — narrow by content rating (PG, PG-13, R, etc.).
  • Sort — by name, year, recently added, or popularity.

A small play icon on a poster means the title is ready to watch immediately. A progress bar at the bottom of a poster shows how far you've watched.

Collections

Movie franchises (e.g., Star Wars, Indiana Jones) are grouped into collections. The collections page shows each franchise with an "owned / total" count. Click into a collection to see which installments you have and which you're missing.

Tags

Tags are color-coded labels applied to titles. Browse all tags to find titles by genre, theme, or any custom grouping your administrator has set up.

Family Videos

Personal and family recordings appear here with event dates, descriptions, and family member tags. Filter by family member or sort by newest, oldest, name, or recently added.

Title Detail

Click any title to see its full detail page, including:

  • Synopsis, release year, content rating, and format (Blu-ray, DVD, etc.)
  • Cast members (click to see their other titles in the library)
  • Episode list with per-episode play buttons (TV shows)
  • Tags and collection membership
  • Similar titles

Watching Videos

The video player opens when you click Play on any title or episode.

Resuming

If you've previously started a video, you'll be asked whether to Resume from where you left off or Start Over. Your position is saved automatically every 60 seconds and whenever you pause.

Player Controls

  • Play / Pause — click the video or the play button.
  • Skip Back 10s / Forward 30s — quick jump buttons.
  • Seek Bar — click anywhere on the timeline to jump. Hover over the bar to see a thumbnail preview of that moment.
  • Subtitles — toggle English subtitles on or off (when available).
  • Fullscreen — double-click the video or use the fullscreen button.

Skip Intro

When chapter data is available, a Skip Intro button appears during the opening credits of TV episodes. Click it to jump past the intro.

Up Next

For TV episodes, when the end credits begin, an Up Next overlay appears with a 10-second countdown to automatically start the next episode. Click Play Now to skip ahead, or Cancel to stay on the current episode.

Chapter Markers

Yellow tick marks on the seek bar indicate chapter boundaries. These help you navigate to specific scenes.

Wish List

The wish list lets you request titles you'd like added to the library. Your wishes are visible to administrators to help inform purchasing decisions.

Adding a Wish

Use the TMDB search bar at the top of the wish list page to find a movie or TV show. Click Add on any search result to add it to your list. You can also add wishes from collection detail pages (heart icon on titles you don't own).

Lifecycle Stages

Each wish moves through stages as it's fulfilled:

  • Wished for — your request has been noted.
  • Ordered — the media has been purchased.
  • In house, pending NAS — the disc has arrived and is being copied.
  • On NAS, pending desktop — the file is on the server, waiting to be transcoded for streaming.
  • Ready to watch — the title is available to stream. Click it to start watching.

A green badge on the My Wish List nav item tells you how many wishes are ready to watch.

Transcode Wishes

If a title is already in the library but not yet transcoded for streaming, you can request it be prioritized. These appear in the Transcode Wishes section of your wish list.

Cameras

The cameras page shows a live grid of all security cameras in the house.

  • Snapshot mode (default) — low-bandwidth still images that refresh every 3 seconds.
  • Live mode — continuous video streams. Uses more bandwidth but shows real-time motion. Toggle with the button in the header.

Click any camera to open a fullscreen view. Click outside the image or press the X to close.

Live TV

Watch over-the-air TV channels streamed from the household's HDHomeRun tuner.

The channel guide shows available channels with their number, call sign, and network affiliation. Click a channel to start watching. There may be a brief delay (5–15 seconds) while the stream starts up.

The number of simultaneous streams is limited by tuner hardware. If all tuners are in use, you'll see an "All tuners are busy" message — try again shortly.

You can filter out low-quality channels by adjusting your minimum quality threshold in your Profile settings.

Profile & Settings

Access your profile from the account icon in the top-right corner.

Account Information

View your username, display name, role, and content rating limit (if one has been set by an administrator).

Live TV Quality

Set the minimum reception quality for channels shown in the Live TV guide. Channels below your threshold are hidden. Use the star rating to adjust (1 = show all channels, 5 = only the strongest signals).

Change Password

Click Change Password to open the password dialog. You'll need your current password and a new password (minimum 8 characters). Changing your password signs out all other sessions for security.

Active Sessions

See all devices where you're currently signed in, including browsers and paired Roku devices. You can revoke individual sessions or sign out of all other sessions at once.
